## One-liner

scubaSeason.fun turns NOAA ocean observations into trip-level decision tools for
divers and dive operators — answering "is this reef healthy, and is now the right
season to go?" with sourced, confidence-rated data.

## The problem

Dive-tourism decisions (a multi-billion-dollar slice of the blue economy) are made
on stale blog posts and operator marketing. Meanwhile authoritative ocean data —
thermal stress, reef condition, sea-surface conditions — sits in NOAA archives that
consumers and small operators can't practically query.

## What we do with NOAA data

We ingest and productize four NOAA feeds per dive location:

- **Coral Reef Watch** → live thermal-stress and bleaching-alert risk (SST anomaly,
  Degree Heating Weeks)
- **CoastWatch / OceanWatch (ERDDAP)** → per-coordinate satellite oceanography (SST,
  chlorophyll-a, salinity, ocean heat)
- **NDBC buoy network** → site-adjacent in-situ wave/wind/water-temp conditions
- **National Coral Reef Monitoring Program (NCRMP)** → observed reef condition

We fuse these into seasonality, reef-health, and bleaching-risk signals across [N]
dive sites, each rendered on an interactive globe with a "Sources & methodology" panel.

## Why it fits the NOAA Ocean Enterprise Initiative

We're a direct commercialization of NOAA's public ocean data — converting it into
economic value for the dive-tourism and marine-recreation economy, exactly the
"data analytics / ecosystem services / environmental monitoring & forecasting"
sectors StartBlue serves. Every recommendation is traceable to a named NOAA (or peer
survey) source with a confidence level and an explicit statement of what the data
can and cannot prove — we separate *observed condition*, *current thermal stress*,
and *projection*, and never publish a projection without a documented model and
uncertainty.
