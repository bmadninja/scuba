**Title:** I built a free nonprofit reef atlas and I would love honest feedback from this community

Hi everyone! I am a diver and I built a free thing I wanted to share, hoping for some honest feedback.

It is called ScubaSeason (scubaseason.fun), a nonprofit reef atlas covering 356 dive sites worldwide. I was so frustrated planning trips off decade old forum threads and blog posts with no idea of actual reef health, so I just built the site I wished existed!

It pulls from 63 live data sources, so every site gets a current reef state: Thriving, Under Pressure, or Witnessing Change, based on coral cover, bleaching alerts, degree heating weeks, and fishing pressure. There is also a species sighting layer showing the probability of spotting specific animals before you book.

A fun one: Tela in Honduras is the only reef in the entire Caribbean labelled as Thriving right now. Just one green dot in a sea of red. I shared it with a friend and she immediately wanted to reprioritize her whole trip list!

I am currently working from public data sources and would love to make it more powerful. If anyone here knows conservation institutes, reef monitoring programs, or research groups who might be open to sharing data, I would genuinely love an introduction!

And please, tell me what is broken or missing. I built this for divers like you and me!
