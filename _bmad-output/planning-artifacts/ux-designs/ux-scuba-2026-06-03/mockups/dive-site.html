Conditions

What to expect in the water

Entry type

Boat

Live-aboard or day boat from Waisai. 30 – 45 min transit.

Current

Mild to moderate

Incoming tide brings mantas to the cleaning station. Plan arrival 30 min before slack.

Visibility

15 – 25 m typical

Can drop to 8 m after heavy rain. Check conditions day-of with your operator.

Water temperature

27 – 29°C

Consistent year-round. 3 mm wetsuit comfortable; shorty fine for most.

Reef health

Excellent

Hard coral cover 74% at Raja Ampat region level. No bleaching alert active.

Photography

Wide angle

Mantas at cleaning station at close range. Blue water background. Strobe optional.

How to dive this site

Sequence and positioning

01

Arrive on the incoming tide

The cleaning station only activates when current brings plankton-rich water across the sand flat. Ask your operator for the tide table the night before — aim to be in the water 20 minutes before the tide turns.

02

Descend to the sandy slope at 10 m

Position yourself on the sandy bottom facing the cleaning station coral head. Stay low and still. Mantas approach from the blue and circle — sudden movements end the encounter.

03

Give mantas a 3 m radius

Manta Sandy has resident mantas that are habituated to divers, but they will abort the cleaning station visit if approached too closely. Watch from the sand, let them come to you.

04

Save bottom time for the wall

After the cleaning station, follow the slope down to 18 m where the reef wall begins. This is where pygmy seahorses live on sea fans and wobbegong sharks rest under overhangs.
