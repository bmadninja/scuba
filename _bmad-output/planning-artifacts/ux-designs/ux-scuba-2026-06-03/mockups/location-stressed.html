January through May. Calmer seas, better visibility — typically 15 – 25 m. Hammerhead aggregations peak at Darwin and Wolf during this window. Currents are strong year-round; all remote sites require Advanced certification.

Getting there

Entry airports

Baltra (GPS) or San Cristóbal (SCY)

Fly via Quito (UIO) or Guayaquil (GYE). Daily connections on LATAM and Avianca.

Remote sites

Live-aboard only

Darwin and Wolf Islands are accessible only by live-aboard vessel — no day-trip boats are permitted. Typical itineraries run 7 – 10 nights departing from Puerto Ayora.

Central sites

Gordon Rocks and Kicker Rock are reachable by day-boat from Puerto Ayora (Gordon Rocks) and Puerto Baquerizo Moreno (Kicker Rock).
