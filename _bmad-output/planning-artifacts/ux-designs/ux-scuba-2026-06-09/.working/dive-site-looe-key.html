Looe Key

Florida Keys · United States

A shallow, sunlit spur and groove reef inside the Looe Key sanctuary, named for a British frigate that wrecked here in 1744. Fishing has been banned for decades, so the coral heads are busy: schools of grunts, the occasional resident goliath grouper, and turtles grazing the flats. One of the easiest world class reef dives in the country.

Getting there ⌄
Fly into Key West (EYW), about 45 minutes by road, or Miami (MIA), roughly 2.5 hours down the Overseas Highway. Operators run out from Big Pine Key and reach the reef in about 30 minutes.
