scubaseason.fun — Citizen Science MVP

DRAFT   Created 2026-06-07

Problem Statement

9–14 million recreational divers enter the ocean every year and observe marine ecosystems that science institutes are struggling to monitor at scale. Those institutes default to expensive robots, remote sensing, and specialist expeditions — not because local expertise is inferior, but because they don't know where to find it and don't trust what they can't credential.

Meanwhile, 78% of divers who would participate in citizen science programs have never been personally invited to one. The platforms that exist (iNaturalist, Reef Check, REEF, eOceans) each capture a slice of this, but none are integrated with where divers actually plan their trips. The feedback loop — showing divers what their data changed — is the #1 retention lever, and only 38% of current participants are satisfied with it.

scubaseason.fun already sits at the highest-intent moment in a diver's journey: site research before a dive. This PRD scopes the MVP that adds a structured citizen science layer — turning site discovery into an observation pipeline — while extending participation to boat operators and dive guides, the local community members most consistently present at reef systems.

diver → structured observation → validation → scientific database (GBIF/OBIS/GCRMN-compatible) → closed feedback loop back to diver

No existing platform delivers this end-to-end.

Goals

  1. Enable recreational divers to submit structured observations (species sightings, debris, water conditions) tied to specific dive sites.
  2. Apply AI-assisted species identification so divers with no taxonomy knowledge can contribute valid data.
  3. Close the feedback loop: show every contributor how their data is being used and what it has revealed.
  4. Extend contribution pathways to boat operators and dive guides — the local community members with the most consistent reef access.
  5. Export observations in Darwin Core Archive (DwC-A) format compatible with GBIF/OBIS/GCRMN for ingestion by science partners.
  6. Reinforce scubaseason.fun's nonprofit identity at every touchpoint: science participation is framed as contribution, not gamification.

Non-Goals (MVP)

  • eDNA collection programs (Phase 3+)
  • Dive computer data integration / Seabed 2030 bathymetry (Phase 3+)
  • Corporate TNFD biodiversity data product (Phase 4)
  • New affiliate surfaces or revenue mechanisms
  • Native iOS/Android app (mobile web only)
  • Real-time bleaching event alerts (Phase 3+)

User Segments

Recreational Divers

Certified divers visiting sites in the scubaseason.fun database. Motivated primarily by intrinsic factors: seeing what they found, knowing it mattered (82% cite field interest; 80% cite meaningful contribution). They have cameras or phones; most lack taxonomy knowledge. They need identification assistance and a visible feedback loop.

Boat Operators and Dive Guides

On the water daily. Observe reef conditions systematically without necessarily being certified scientists. They represent the "local expertise" that science institutes currently cannot locate or trust. ASSUMPTION: boat operators are the primary local community segment for MVP; fishermen and village-level guides are Phase 2+

Science Partners (read-only at MVP)

Marine research institutes, reef monitoring networks (GCRMN, GBIF, OBIS contributors), and NGOs seeking observation data from under-monitored shallow-water sites. Active partnership agreements and co-branding are Phase 2.

Features

F1 — Dive Site Observation Submission

A structured form linked to each dive site page allowing divers (and boat operators) to log an observation from a specific site visit.

FR-1.1 Every dive site page must include an "Add observation" entry point visible without login. On tap/click, the user is prompted to sign in or create a free account before submitting.
FR-1.2 The observation form must capture:
  • Site (pre-filled from the dive site page)
  • Date of dive (date picker, defaults to today)
  • Depth range (shallow / mid / deep)
  • Water visibility (poor / moderate / good / excellent)
  • Water temperature (°C or °F, optional)
  • Species observed (multi-select with AI assist)
  • Debris observed (yes/no; if yes: type + quantity)
  • Photo upload (optional; up to 5 images, max 10MB each)
  • Notes (free text, 500 char max, optional)
FR-1.3 Observations are stored with a unique ID, hashed contributor ID, site ID, and ISO 8601 timestamp.
FR-1.4 Submission confirmation must include: observation ID, a one-sentence summary of what was logged, and a link to the contributor's observation history.
FR-1.5 Boat operators and dive guides can self-identify during account creation. Their observations are flagged as higher-confidence for site-condition fields.

F2 — AI-Assisted Species Identification

Photo upload triggers an AI inference call that suggests the most likely species matches; the diver confirms or corrects.

FR-2.1 Photo upload triggers an AI species ID API call (CoralNet Deploy for coral/invertebrates; Fishial.AI for fish ASSUMPTION: confirmed at architecture phase) returning up to 3 candidate species with confidence scores.
FR-2.2 Suggestions display common name, scientific name, and confidence %. Suggestions below 50% show a visible "uncertain" label.
FR-2.3 The diver can: accept a suggestion, reject all and type manually, or mark as "unknown species." All three outcomes are stored.
FR-2.5 Species suggestions are filtered to the biogeographic region of the dive site to reduce implausible results.
FR-2.6 AI inference must complete within 8 seconds or gracefully degrade — diver can skip to manual entry without losing form state.

F3 — Contribution Feedback Loop

Every contributor can see the aggregate impact of their data — and the community's data — at the site and species level.

FR-3.1 Each dive site page must display a "Community data" panel:
  • Total observations at this site
  • Top 5 species (last 12 months)
  • Last observation date
  • Debris report rate (% of recent observations with debris)
FR-3.2 Contributor profile page shows: total observations, species recorded (unique), sites visited (unique), and a monthly "contribution impact" sentence (e.g. "Your 12 observations included 3 species not previously recorded at this site").
FR-3.3 When a contributor records a species not previously logged at a site, the site profile is updated and the contributor is notified.
FR-3.4 A public "Science impact" page shows site-wide aggregate numbers and, once exports occur, count of records sent to partner databases.
NFR-3.1 Contribution metrics must be framed around discovery and impact — not points, badges, or leaderboards. Competitive gamification language is prohibited.

F4 — Boat Operator and Dive Guide Onboarding

A lightweight onboarding flow for local operators that surfaces their site-specific knowledge and makes their observations higher-value in the data pipeline.

FR-4.1 Operators selecting the appropriate role see a short (3–5 question) profile: primary operating site(s), years of experience, formal marine biology or survey training.
FR-4.2 Operator profiles are not publicly visible at MVP. ASSUMPTION: public profiles and trust badges are Phase 2
FR-4.3 Operator observation form includes one extra field: "Unusual conditions" free text (300 char) for anomalies (unexpected bleaching, storm damage, unusual species).
FR-4.4 Operators can opt in to a monthly site digest email: all community observations at their primary site(s), including new species records.

F5 — Science Data Export Pipeline

Structured export of validated observations in Darwin Core Archive format for ingestion by GBIF, OBIS, and GCRMN-compatible systems.

FR-5.1 Observations stored in a schema mapping to Darwin Core fields: occurrenceID, basisOfRecord (HumanObservation), decimalLatitude/Longitude, eventDate, scientificName, vernacularName, identificationRemarks, recordedBy (hashed), datasetName, institutionCode, license.
FR-5.2 Validation gate before export eligibility:
  • Date must be in the past
  • Site coordinates must resolve to a known scubaseason.fun site
  • Species field must be non-empty
  • At least one other field (depth, visibility, temperature, or debris) must be filled
FR-5.3 DwC-A export endpoint for authenticated science partners (API key + signed agreement). Returns validated observations as a ZIP with occurrence.txt and meta.xml.
FR-5.4 Observation records licensed under CC BY 4.0 (GBIF-eligible). Uploaded photos licensed CC BY-NC 4.0. Contributors explicitly accept both on first submission.
FR-5.5 No PII in exports. Contributor IDs are hashed. ASSUMPTION: GDPR-adequate hashing; legal review at launch
NFR-5.1 Science partner agreements are manual at MVP (email + API key). Automated onboarding is Phase 2.

F6 — Reef Status Education Layer

Contextual information on each dive site page explaining what science knows (and doesn't know) about that reef.

FR-6.1 Each dive site page must display the reef status indicator: Thriving / Under pressure / Witnessing change. (Field already exists in the data model; surface it prominently.)
FR-6.2 Each reef status links to a 200–300 word explanation: what is happening, what monitoring shows, what the diver can do.
FR-6.3 A "Why this matters" module on each site page explains the Seabed 2030 context in plain language: shallow-water reef systems are among the least-mapped areas of the ocean; diver observations fill a real gap; scubaseason.fun routes data to scientific databases.
FR-6.4 Sites with fewer than 3 observations in the last 12 months display a "Help us learn more about this site" CTA linking to the observation form.

Non-Functional Requirements

NFR-P1 — Performance Observation form loads within 2 seconds on 4G. AI species ID completes within 8 seconds or gracefully degrades.
NFR-S1 — Privacy No PII in science exports. Contributor email never shared with partners. GDPR-compliant for EU users.
NFR-A1 — Accessibility Observation form meets WCAG 2.1 AA. Photo upload includes alt-text prompt.
NFR-M1 — Mobile-first Observation form fully functional on mobile browser. Camera/gallery upload works on iOS Safari and Android Chrome.
NFR-D1 — Data quality Invalid observations stored as pending_validation, not deleted. Not included in exports or aggregates until resolved.
NFR-T1 — Nonprofit tone No points, levels, or leaderboards. All copy reflects scientific contribution and community stewardship. Language review required before launch.
