## PAGE 3 — TECHNOLOGY AND PRODUCT

**Describe your technology, product, or service.**

Scuba Season is an ocean data platform that gives scuba divers a real, science-backed reef health rating for every dive site before they book a trip. We aggregate data from 63 verified sources including Global Fishing Watch fishing pressure data, coral cover surveys, iNaturalist species observations, and GBIF occurrence records to produce 3 reef state labels: Thriving, Under Pressure, and Witnessing Change. Every site on Scuba Season shows 1 real data point alongside a reef trajectory so divers understand whether a reef is getting better or worse over time, not just what it looks like today.

We also surface species probability for each site using live iNaturalist and GBIF ingest, so a diver planning a manta ray trip to Nusa Penida or a thresher shark dive in Malapascua knows what the actual observation data says rather than relying on operator marketing.

The platform is live at scubaseason.fun.

---

**How does your technology, product, or service support the goals of the NOAA Ocean Enterprise Initiative?**

Scuba Season is a consumer distribution layer for ocean observation data. The NOAA Ocean Enterprise Initiative is designed to grow the blue economy by making ocean data accessible and commercially useful, and we do exactly that for the 6 to 7 million active scuba divers worldwide.

Right now, ocean health data sits in research repositories that the average diver will never find. We translate that data into a format that a person planning a dive trip can actually act on. When a diver chooses a Thriving reef over a degraded one because Scuba Season told them the difference, that is a real behavioral shift driven by open ocean data! That shift rewards operators who protect their reefs, creates demand for more rigorous monitoring, and puts ocean observation data at the center of a $11B global dive travel market.

We are also designed to grow with new data sources. Every new NOAA dataset, every new partner observation network, is something we can ingest and surface to divers immediately. The pipeline is built for it.

---

**What problem are you solving, and who experiences it?**

Scuba divers spend thousands of dollars on dive trips and have almost no reliable way to evaluate reef health before they go. The information that exists is scattered across scientific papers, operator websites with obvious marketing bias, and travel blogs written years ago. There is no single place to answer the question every diver actually has: is the reef still good?

At the same time, marine scientists and conservation organizations are generating extraordinary amounts of ocean observation data that almost never reaches the people whose travel decisions could amplify conservation impact the most. Divers are a uniquely motivated audience! They are already choosing to get in the water and see reefs up close. They just need the right information to make decisions that support reef health rather than inadvertently pile pressure onto already degraded sites.

Scuba Season closes that gap.

---

**What is your target market?**

Primary: recreational scuba divers planning international dive trips. There are 6 to 7 million active certified divers globally, with an estimated $11B in annual dive travel spend. These are people already investing significantly in ocean experiences, highly motivated by reef quality, and willing to change their itinerary based on good information.

Secondary: dive operators and liveaboard companies who want to compete on reef quality rather than just price. Operators at healthy, well-monitored reefs have no credible way to prove that right now. Scuba Season gives them a data-backed signal they can point to.

Tertiary: marine conservation organizations seeking consumer-facing distribution for reef data and a behavioral channel into the dive travel market.

---

**What is your business model?**

Scuba Season is a nonprofit. We are building revenue through 3 paths:

1. Affiliate partnerships with dive operators and booking platforms. Operators at Thriving reefs pay a small referral fee when divers book through Scuba Season.

2. Conservation-aligned grants and non-dilutive funding. The NOAA Ocean Enterprise Award from StartBlue would be our first significant grant, and we are actively seeking similar funding from ocean-focused foundations.

3. Data licensing to research partners who want aggregated, consumer-level behavioral data on how divers are engaging with reef health information.

We are not raising equity funding. Every dollar goes back into data infrastructure and expanding site coverage.

**Who are your competitors, and what makes Scuba Season different?**

The closest alternatives are general dive trip review sites like DiveBuddy, Diviac, and parts of TripAdvisor. None of these use real ocean health data. Their reef descriptions are user-submitted reviews, not science, and they have no way to tell a diver whether a reef is in decline.

A few conservation dashboards like Coral Triangle Initiative and GCRMN publish reef health data but are built for researchers, not travelers, and have no connection to trip planning.

Scuba Season is the only platform that sits at the intersection of ocean science and dive travel planning. We aggregate the data, translate it into plain language, and put it directly in front of people who are about to make a booking decision.
