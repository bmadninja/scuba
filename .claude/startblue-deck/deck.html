The Problem 02

Divers spend thousands on a trip with no reliable way to know a reef's current condition.

  • The science exists, but it lives in NOAA feeds, survey databases, and journal papers the diving community never sees.
  • Operator marketing fills the gap with outdated or biased claims.
  • 6 to 7 million divers make booking and in water decisions every year, effectively blind.

A live web atlas for every dive site.

We fuse NOAA Coral Reef Watch satellite data with in water surveys to label each reef, and we calculate species encounter probability from real diver logs.

Why Now · The Ocean Enterprise Loop 06

NOAA needs ocean data to reach beyond research institutions, and to gather more observations. We do both.

  • We distribute NOAA Coral Reef Watch data and 62 other sources to millions of motivated divers in plain language.
  • We turn those divers into a distributed sensor network, forwarding their sightings to iNaturalist, GBIF, and OBIS automatically.
  • We both consume Ocean Enterprise data and generate new observations for it, and we ingest every new NOAA dataset as it publishes.

Where We Are Going 08

Connecting two sides that rarely meet.

Conservation organizations and research institutes spend heavily sending teams on expeditions to monitor reefs. Yet millions of divers are already in that same water every day.

We are building the structured layer between them, so organizations can commission monitoring and pay divers for verified observations instead of funding costly expeditions.
